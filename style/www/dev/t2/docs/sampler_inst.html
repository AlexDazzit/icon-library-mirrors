Icon usage in Tigris

The techniques shown here (view source) make no use of the img tag, keeping verbose details like height, width, spacing, border treatments, and pathname of images out of HTML and in the style system, where they can be managed most cleanly. Beat the rush and retire img before XHTML 2 scraps it formally.

Two styles of tree browser

In this first group of 4, the icon and text are integral (same link or other element). CVSWeb's UI works this way.

filebrowse leaf

filebrowse leafnode/

filebrowse expanded/

filebrowse collapsed/

In this second group, the icon and text are separate links, with the icon having a textual fallback (for Nav4 and other special cases). Nidaba (file-sharing component of SourceCast) works this way.

[file] filebrowse-alt leaf

/ filebrowse-alt leafnode

/- filebrowse-alt expanded

/+ filebrowse-alt collapsed

To represent logical nesting, use ul/li:

  • filebrowse expanded/
    • filebrowse leaf
    • filebrowse leaf
  • filebrowse expanded/
    • filebrowse expanded/
      • filebrowse leaf
    • filebrowse collapsed/

But wait there's more

CSS came late to the TLA party. It competes with Christ Saves Souls, Certified Semen Services, and Closed Source Software, but Cascading Style Sheets is more than a religion or animal husbandry commodity: much more. It is a way out of the bloated, murky HTML generation and maintenance business. It is a way to cut the transmission times of your screens to perhaps 30% what they are now, depending on how bad you've been. It is a way to stop using HTML tables except for train schedule-style tabular data. No more font tags, no more spacer GIFs, no more GIFs of text. And still look good - better even than with all the painful crufty techniques just mentioned.
